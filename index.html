1. Abstract

Recent advancements in text-to-speech (TTS) have significantly improved emotional speech synthesis. However, many existing approaches capture only averaged emotional representations, thereby failing to reflect the inherently dynamic nature of speech prosody. To address these issues, we propose STMH, a novel framework designed to enhance the expressiveness and controllability of emotional speech synthesis. STMH integrates an emotion condition module, which captures global emotion representations and allows simultaneous control over both emotion category and intensity, as well as an emotion harmonization module to harmonization the condition mismatch between the training and inference. In addition, a mel-derivative augmented conditional adversarial framework is used to enhance emotional consistency and improve synthesis quality. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach surpasses state-of-the-art methods in terms of emotional expressiveness and controllability.
